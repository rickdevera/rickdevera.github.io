About

Principal Solutions Architect.
Field-focused technologist.

I work at the intersection of cloud security, infrastructure, and AI — focused on building and validating the controls that organizations actually depend on. My background spans network security, identity and access management, infrastructure automation, and CNAPP strategy across AWS, GCP, and Azure. At Tenable, I serve as Technical Alliance Architect for cloud providers, leading technical enablement and working directly with cloud platform architects to develop integration patterns and lab-backed solutions.

Earlier in my career I worked at Check Point in roles from Security Engineer through Regional Director, and before that as a Senior Software Engineering Lead at Lockheed Martin and Software Engineer at United Space Alliance — where my foundation in systems thinking and rigorous testing was built. I've been a practitioner first throughout, and I believe architecture is only valuable when it can be implemented and validated in the real world.

How I Work
Lab First

Before publishing or recommending anything, I build it. Lab environments aren't for demo purposes — they're where assumptions get tested against reality.

Honest Documentation

Notes include what didn't work. The edge cases and failure modes are often more valuable than the happy path.

Enablement Over Hype

Workshops, walkthroughs, and architecture guides are written for practitioners who need to execute — not for audiences who just need to be impressed.

Continuously Revising

Cloud security and AI don't sit still. What held up last year may not hold up today. I revisit old recommendations regularly.

Outside the screen I build things with my hands — 3D printing, woodworking, DIY, and golf. The same instincts apply: measure carefully, test before committing, and don't ignore what the material is telling you.
