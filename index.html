About

Architect, practitioner, and lifelong student of systems.

I work as a Cloud Security Architect with a focus on building and validating the controls that organizations depend on to operate safely in cloud-native environments. My background spans network security, identity and access management, infrastructure automation, and—more recently—the governance and enablement challenges that come with deploying AI systems at scale. I've spent more time in infrastructure terminals than in slide decks, and I believe the most durable technical knowledge comes from building things that fail in ways you didn't predict.

Over the past several years, my work has increasingly intersected with AI—not the hype, but the practical questions organizations face when integrating AI into workflows that carry real risk. How do you establish access boundaries for an autonomous agent? What does audit logging look like when the actor is a language model? These are engineering problems, and I approach them the way I approach cloud security: start with threat modeling, build controls, test them, revise. This site documents the work in progress.

How I Work
Lab First

Before writing or recommending anything, I build it. Lab environments exist so that assumptions can be tested against reality rather than documented around it.

Documented Honestly

Notes include what didn't work. Failure modes are often more informative than success paths, and practitioners deserve accurate accounts of both.

Slowly and Thoroughly

I publish infrequently. Each post or lab represents something I've spent enough time with to have genuine confidence in what I'm sharing.

Without Affiliations

No sponsored content, no vendor relationships, no affiliate links. The assessments here are based on what I've run in my own environments.

Outside of cloud and security, I build things with my hands—3D printing, woodworking, and the occasional DIY project. There's a shared discipline in both domains: measure carefully, test before committing, and don't ignore what the material is telling you.
